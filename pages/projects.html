Comparative Performance of Machine Learning Algorithms for Malicious Traffic Classification in Wireless Networks

An academic research thesis (co-authored with L. F. López Kú) evaluating three machine learning and deep learning architectures (Random Forest, LightGBM, and CNNs) for classifying link-layer attacks in IEEE 802.11 WLANs. Trained on the AWID3 dataset, the research applies traffic statistics feature engineering (frame length, inter-arrival times), 10-fold cross-validation, and SHAP explainability. It focuses on optimizing the trade-off between high classification accuracy (F1-score ≥ 95%) and ultra-low latency (< 1 ms) for edge computing hardware deployment.
